# CASE_D4 Language and Translation Memo

## Language composition
- **1 fully English transcript**: HWEL10AR
- **4 Arabic-dominant mixed-language transcripts**: HWEL1AR, HWEL3AR, HWEL7AR, HWEL9AR
- **Primarily Arabic note files**: HWEL1NT1, HWEL3NT1, HWEL4NT1, HWEL6NT1, HWEL7NT1
- **Primarily English note files**: HWEL10NT1, HWEL10NT2
- **Recommendation workbook**: Health_Workshop_Suggestions Day 4.xlsx (auxiliary only)

## Translation handling rules

### Arabic as authoritative where Arabic is original
Arabic is the authoritative source language for HWEL1AR, HWEL3AR, HWEL7AR, and HWEL9AR. Where Arabic text is quoted or coded, the Arabic remains the primary evidentiary form. English glosses or translations are interpretive aids for reporting only.

### English sources are natively authoritative in their own right
HWEL10AR is a natively English source and should be quoted directly where the cleaned wording is clearly speaker-attributed. The English note files remain authoritative as note-taker summaries of their own wording, but they are still note-derived contextual material rather than transcript speech.

### Mixed-language handling
Several Day 4 Arabic transcripts contain embedded English terms such as `holistic`, `home care`, `tracking`, `MDT`, `I-COPE`, `patient-centered care`, and similar professional or institutional vocabulary. These should not be normalized away during coding. The mixed-language form is part of how participants actually articulated elderly-health concepts.

## Key Arabic concepts preserved

| Arabic term | Transliteration | Working gloss | Why preserved |
|------------|----------------|---------------|---------------|
| الحياة الطيبة | al-hayat al-tayyiba | the good / wholesome life | Carries moral-spiritual, relational, and ethical meaning beyond a thin wellbeing label. |
| العافية | al-'afiya | wellness / wholeness / being well | Often exceeds narrow biomedical health and includes social, psychological, and spiritual integrity. |
| كبار القدر | kibar al-qadr | elders / people of standing | Preferred dignity-framing term in the Day 4 discussion and analytically important in how ageing is positioned. |
| كبار السن | kibar al-sinn | older adults / elderly people | Used in some sources, often contrasted with the more dignity-oriented كبار القدر. |
| كرامة | karama | dignity | Central Day 4 concept for how care should be delivered to older people. |
| احتواء | ihtiwa' | containment / holding / inclusion | Repeated Day 4 term for the social and emotional holding of older people. |
| رعاية متمحورة حول الفرد | ri'aya mutamarkiza hawl al-fard | person-centered care | Important service-design phrase in the home-care and rehabilitation discussion. |

## Semantic caution around العافية
Day 4 includes explicit discussion that العافية can carry different regional meanings, including the Moroccan usage where the word may index fire in some idiomatic contexts (HWEL7AR). That semantic variability is analytically relevant and should be preserved rather than silently standardized.

## Note-file language asymmetry
The note files are not literal translations of transcript speech. Even where an English note file exists for an English transcript, it must still be treated as note-taker summary material rather than as line-perfect participant wording.

## Recommendation workbook language handling
The recommendation workbook contains Arabic and English suggestions, but because it is auxiliary structured material rather than transcript evidence, language issues here are governed by the recommendation-usage rule rather than by quotation authority.

## Reporting convention
In later reporting outputs:
- Arabic quotations should be presented in Arabic with English translation or gloss where needed
- English quotations from HWEL10AR should be presented directly
- Mixed-language wording should be preserved where it reflects how participants actually spoke
- No silent back-translation from English into Arabic or vice versa

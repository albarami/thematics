# CASE_D4 Boundary Memo

## Purpose
This memo defines the analytic scope, evidence hierarchy, and reporting limits for CASE_D4 (Day 4 — Elderly) before coding begins.

---

## 1. Analytic scope

### In scope
- Day 4 transcripts: HWEL1AR, HWEL3AR, HWEL7AR, HWEL9AR, HWEL10AR
- Day 4 note files: HWEL1NT1, HWEL3NT1, HWEL4NT1, HWEL6NT1, HWEL7NT1, HWEL10NT1, HWEL10NT2
- Day 4 recommendation workbook (auxiliary only): Health_Workshop_Suggestions Day 4.xlsx
- Seven discussion questions (Q1–Q7) as documented in `CASE_D4_question_map.md`

### Out of scope
- All other day-cases (D1, D2, D3, D5)
- Cross-case synthesis or comparison
- Any Day 5 / PolicyMakers material
- Any material not in the Day 4 raw folder or Day 4 recommendation workbook

---

## 2. Source and speaker-type boundaries

### Source hierarchy
1. **Primary verbal evidence**: Transcripts (HWEL1AR, HWEL3AR, HWEL7AR, HWEL9AR, HWEL10AR)
2. **Contextual triangulation**: Note files (all seven Day 4 note files)
3. **Auxiliary structured material**: Recommendation workbook

### Speaker-type boundaries
- **Participant speech** is the primary evidence base for coding, themes, and quotation
- **Moderator speech** is retained for context counts but excluded from participant-evidence use, theme support, and quotation diversity
- **Note-taker summary** material is contextual triangulation, not transcript-equivalent participant speech
- **Unclear** attribution material may be retained with caution but must not inflate participant diversity

### Note-only table limits
- Tables 4 and 6 are note-only sources and cannot generate transcript-equivalent evidence, participant quotations, or participant-diversity claims
- They remain valuable for contextual triangulation and for confirming the seven-question structure

### Transcript-only table limits
- Table 9 (HWEL9AR) has no paired note file and therefore lacks note-based triangulation
- This must be stated explicitly rather than silently smoothed over in later reporting

---

## 3. Evidence hierarchy for coding and theme development

| Evidence type | Use in coding | Use in themes | Use in quotation |
|--------------|---------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Transcript participant speech | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Transcript moderator speech | Context only | No | No |
| Note-taker summary | Contextual triangulation | Contextual support only | No (unless labeled) |
| Recommendation workbook | No | No | No |

---

## 4. Question and theme boundaries

- Themes must be developed from the coded transcript evidence, not from keyword matching
- Question-to-theme claims must be grounded in coded segments, not assumed from question wording alone
- The recommendation workbook may support Q6/Q7 reporting when explicitly labeled as auxiliary material, but it must not generate themes independently

---

## 5. Reporting limits

- CASE_D4 reporting must not make cross-case claims or comparisons
- CASE_D4 reporting must not introduce Day 5 material
- CASE_D4 reporting must not present moderator speech as participant evidence
- CASE_D4 reporting must not present note-taker summaries as verbatim participant wording without explicit labeling
- CASE_D4 reporting must acknowledge the source asymmetry (note-only tables 4 and 6, transcript-only table 9) rather than treating all tables as equivalent
- CASE_D4 reporting must preserve the culturally specific Arabic terminology documented in `CASE_D4_language_memo.md` rather than flattening it into generic English equivalents

---

## 6. Stop rule
After CASE_D4 is complete through all seven gates, work stops. The combined final report across cases is not started until explicitly requested.

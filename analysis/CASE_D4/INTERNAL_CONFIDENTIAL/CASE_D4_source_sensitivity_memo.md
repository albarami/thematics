## Cross-source sensitivity issues

1. **Source asymmetry**: Tables 4 and 6 are note-only, while table 9 is transcript-only.
2. **Provider voice dominance**: The clearest Day 4 evidence is strongly provider-heavy even when some speakers also position themselves as family members or service users.
3. **Compression risk in English**: `HWEL10AR` preserves a usable but summary-style English record rather than a highly granular transcript.
4. **Label-noise risk in Arabic**: `HWEL7AR` includes late-arrival and short-form labels that should be consolidated carefully rather than treated as certainty by default.
5. **Question-boundary asymmetry**: `HWEL7AR`, `HWEL9AR`, and the note files preserve the clearest Q4-Q7 movement, while `HWEL1AR` needs heavier note support after Q3.
6. **Dignity-language sensitivity**: Terms such as `كبار القدر`, `العافية`, `كرامة`, and `احتواء` carry culturally specific meaning and should not be flattened into narrow English equivalents during coding or reporting.

# CASE_D1 Boundary Memo

## Analytic boundaries for CASE_D1

### Case boundary
- This analysis covers CASE_D1 (Day 1 — Childhood) only.
- No cross-case comparisons with D2-D4 are permitted at this stage.
- No whole-project synthesis is attempted.

### Source boundary
- **Primary verbal data**: 7 transcripts (HWCH0AR, HWCH2AR, HWCH3AR, HWCH4AR, HWCH6AR, HWCH7AR, HWCH10AR)
- **Contextual triangulation**: 8 note files (HWCH0NT1, HWCH2NT1, HWCH3NT2, HWCH4NT1, HWCH5NT1, HWCH6NT3, HWCH7NT1, HWCH10NT1)
- **Auxiliary recommendation material**: Health_Workshop_Suggestions Day 1.xlsx (Q6-Q7 support only, explicitly labeled)
- **Excluded**: _$CH0NT1.docx (temp/lock file)

### Speaker-type boundary
- **participant**: Substantive contributor. Evidence counts toward themes, quotation diversity, and prominence.
- **moderator**: Facilitator/session manager. Contextual use only. Must NEVER count as participant evidence, quotation support, participant summary content, theme evidence, or prominence support.
- **note_taker_summary**: Note-taker's condensed interpretation. Contextual triangulation only. Not verbatim participant speech.
- **unclear**: Speaker whose role cannot be determined from available evidence. Coded content is included but flagged. Does not count toward participant diversity metrics.

### Evidence hierarchy
1. **Transcript participant speech** — strongest evidence. Supports themes, quotations, prominence.
2. **Transcript unclear-speaker content** — included with attribution caveat. Supports themes cautiously.
3. **Note-taker summaries** — contextual support. Confirms or triangulates transcript evidence. Does not independently establish themes.
4. **Auxiliary recommendation workbook** — supplementary tables for Q6-Q7 only. Must be explicitly labeled. Does not support themes.
5. **Moderator speech** — context for understanding discussion flow. Never evidence.

### Theme development boundary
- Themes must emerge from the Day 1 data as encountered in this fresh analysis.
- The old CASE_D1 theme structure from previous passes is not imported or forced.
- Theme names, boundaries, and definitions must be determined by the evidence, not by prior expectations.
- Hidden distress / unreliable appearances may emerge as a full theme, a subtheme, or a cross-cutting pattern depending on evidence strength.

### Question boundary
- Analysis is structured by Q1-Q7 but themes may cut across questions.
- Question-by-theme mapping must be evidence-based, not assumed.
- If a question has no evidence for a theme, it must not be claimed.

### Reporting boundary
- The final report covers CASE_D1 only.
- No claims about "the project" or "all stakeholders" are permitted.
- Findings are situated within the childhood stakeholder context.
- Professional voice dominance is acknowledged as a methodological limitation.

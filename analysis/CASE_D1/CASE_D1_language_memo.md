# CASE_D1 Language and Translation Memo

## Language composition
- **6 Arabic transcripts**: HWCH0AR, HWCH2AR, HWCH3AR, HWCH4AR, HWCH6AR, HWCH7AR
- **1 English transcript**: HWCH10AR
- **6 Arabic note files**: HWCH2NT1, HWCH3NT2, HWCH4NT1, HWCH5NT1, HWCH6NT3, HWCH7NT1
- **2 English note files**: HWCH0NT1, HWCH10NT1
- **1 Arabic recommendation workbook**: Health_Workshop_Suggestions Day 1.xlsx

## Translation handling rules

### Arabic as authoritative
Arabic is the original language for 6 of 7 transcripts and for the recommendation workbook. Where Arabic text is quoted or coded, the Arabic is treated as the authoritative version. English translations or glosses are provided for reporting purposes only and do not override the Arabic source.

### Key Arabic concepts preserved
The following terms carry specific cultural-spiritual meaning that resists direct English translation. They are preserved in Arabic with explanatory glosses:

| Arabic term | Transliteration | Working gloss | Why preserved |
|------------|----------------|---------------|---------------|
| الحياة الطيبة | al-hayat al-tayyiba | the good/wholesome life | Quranic term with spiritual resonance beyond "wellbeing" |
| قناعة | qana'a | contentment / sufficiency | Implies spiritual acceptance, not mere satisfaction |
| رضا | rida | acceptance / satisfaction with divine decree | Theological concept, not psychological satisfaction |
| توازن | tawazun | balance / equilibrium | Used consistently across transcripts for multidimensional balance |
| مدير الجلسة | mudir al-jalsa | session manager | Moderator role label in Arabic transcripts |
| مداخلة | mudakhala | intervention / contribution | Generic label for participant turns in some transcripts |
| ركائز | raka'iz | pillars | Refers to the HT (Hayat Tayyiba) wellbeing pillars |

### English transcript (HWCH10AR)
HWCH10AR is natively in English. No translation is needed. Quotations are used directly. When comparing concepts across Arabic and English transcripts, the comparison is noted explicitly.

### Cross-language note files
HWCH0NT1 is an English note file for the Arabic transcript HWCH0AR. The note-taker's English summaries are treated as interpretive summaries, not as translations of specific Arabic utterances. They are coded as note_taker_summary with the language difference documented.

### Reporting convention
In the final report:
- Arabic quotations are presented in Arabic with English translation in parentheses or footnotes
- Key Arabic terms are preserved with transliteration and gloss on first use
- English quotations from HWCH10AR are presented directly
- The language source is indicated for every quotation

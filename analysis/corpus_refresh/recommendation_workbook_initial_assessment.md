## Initial structural reading

The rows do not preserve named participant identities. Instead, they use generic user placeholders. Some entries are long, cleaned recommendation strings that bundle multiple proposals into a single row. Earlier rows resemble individual suggestion statements, while later content also includes manually collected or aggregated notes. This indicates that the workbook is not a raw transcript-equivalent source.

## Are the rows raw, grouped, or cleaned?
**Provisional judgement**: the rows appear **grouped / cleaned**, not raw workshop speech.

Reasons:
- speaker labels are generic (`User X`) rather than named transcript speakers
- several rows combine multiple recommendations into a single structured entry
- the workbook uses a compact suggestion format rather than conversational turns
- the content is explicitly recommendation-oriented rather than discussion-flow oriented
- manually aggregated notes appear to be present in the sheet structure

## Can rows be tied to questions or speakers?

- **Speakers**: direct speaker-level traceability is weak. Generic `User` labels do not provide a reliable bridge back to named transcript speakers.
- **Quotations**: the workbook should not be treated as a quotation source unless later row-level crosswalk evidence is produced.

## Provisional treatment recommendation
The Day 1 recommendation workbook should be treated as:

- **usable for recommendation / policy / implementation sections**
- **usable most safely for Q7 and secondarily Q6**

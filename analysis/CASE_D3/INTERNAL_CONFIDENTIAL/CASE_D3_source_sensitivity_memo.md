# CASE_D3 Source Sensitivity Memo

## Purpose
This memo documents source-level quality, limitations, and handling decisions that affect how each Day 3 source should be used during familiarisation, coding, and later theme development.

---

## Source-level sensitivity notes

### HWAD1AR (Table 1, Arabic, 67,061 chars)
- **Speaker attribution**: Moderate. The moderator is clearly named in the opening and two participants are repeatedly identifiable by name and role.
- **Moderator separation**: Strong. The opening explicitly names the moderator and later moderation remains recoverable.
- **Participant profile**: Narrow but conceptually strong. The recoverable participant set is small, which limits speaker spread but not analytic depth.
- **Sensitivity note**: The source is highly useful for conceptual and moral-spiritual framing, but it does not offer broad participant diversity.
- **Use value**: High for conceptual discussion and moral-spiritual framing; moderate for later participant-diversity claims.

### HWAD3AR (Table 3, Arabic mixed with English, 53,773 chars)
- **Speaker attribution**: Mixed. Several short-name speakers are recoverable (`د. مها`, `د. حنان`, `د. داليا`, `أ. وضحى`, `أ. آمنة`, `أ. أميرة`), but substantial discussion remains only partly attributable.
- **Moderator separation**: Partial. One generic `المحاور` turn is preserved, but most facilitation remains embedded in flowing text.
- **Sensitivity note**: The transcript is analytically valuable for adult-care challenges, systems language, and practical discussion, but should not be overused for confident participant-breadth claims.
- **Handling rule**: Preserve named short-label speech as participant evidence where stable, and retain unattributed flowing material as `unclear`.
- **Use value**: Medium-High for substantive content; Medium-Low for precise participant-level diversity claims.

### HWAD4AR (Table 4, Arabic-led mixed transcript, 69,586 chars)
- **Speaker attribution**: Good. `مدير الجلسة` is explicit and several participant roles are preserved clearly enough to support named/role-labelled analysis.
- **Moderator separation**: Strong. This is a moderator-heavy source and facilitation can be cleanly separated from participant evidence.
- **Participant mix**: Broad compared with the other Arabic transcripts, including psychology, nursing, research, laboratory, dietetic, midwifery, and academic roles.
- **Sensitivity note**: OCR noise, punctuation instability, and title variation require label consolidation before coding.
- **Use value**: Very high for service-delivery discussion, adult-care implementation talk, and structured question progression.

### HWAD6AR (Table 6, Arabic mixed with English, 84,962 chars)
- **Speaker attribution**: Poor. Only one stable named participant label (`د. وليد`) is recoverable from the extracted transcript.
- **Moderator separation**: Weak. The transcript clearly contains facilitation and question prompting, but no stable moderator label is preserved.
- **Sensitivity note**: The source contains strong systems and adult-care content, yet attribution is too weak for broad participant-level claims.
- **Handling rule**: Use with caution for substantive content; preserve most material as source-level or `unclear` evidence unless speaker recovery is genuinely defensible.
- **Use value**: Medium for content, low for participant-level precision.

### HWAD10AR (Table 10, English, 92,292 chars)
- **Speaker attribution**: Excellent. `Speaker 1`-`Speaker 8` and `Moderator` are stable throughout most of the transcript.
- **Moderator separation**: Strong. Moderator turns are explicit and removable from participant evidence.
- **Participant profile**: Mixed adult professional roles including social work, occupational therapy, mental-health administration, medical training, and private-sector practice.
- **Sensitivity note**: The transcript opens with warm-up or icebreaker material before the actual wellbeing questions. That pre-question material should not be miscoded as though it belonged to the seven-question adult discussion guide.
- **Additional note**: One `Translator` turn is preserved. It may support contextual interpretation around multilingual care, but it is not ordinary participant evidence by default.
- **Use value**: Very high. Best Day 3 source for clean participant-level evidence across the full seven-question structure.

### Note files (contextual triangulation)
- The Day 3 note files follow a standardised Q1-Q7 template and are the strongest source for confirming the canonical seven-question structure across the case.
- `HWAD0NT1.docx`, `HWAD5NT1.docx`, `HWAD7NT1.docx`, `HWAD9NT1.docx`, `HWAD11NT2.docx`, and `HWAD11NT3.docx` are note-only sources with no paired transcript. They are contextually valuable but cannot substitute for primary verbal evidence.
- `HWAD10NT1.docx` and `HWAD10NTI.docx` are especially useful for confirming the English Day 3 question structure and contextual participant rostering, but they remain note-derived summary material rather than transcript evidence.
- `HWAD1NT1.docx`, `HWAD1NT2.docx`, and `HWAD3NT1.docx` are important for confirming question progression where transcript boundaries blur.
- Note content remains `note_taker_summary` material, not verbatim participant speech.

### Recommendation workbook (auxiliary)

## Cross-source sensitivity issues

1. **Attribution inequality**: `HWAD10AR` and `HWAD4AR` provide much stronger speaker attribution than `HWAD6AR` and parts of `HWAD3AR`.

2. **Note-only table asymmetry**: Tables `0`, `5`, `7`, `9`, and `11` expand contextual coverage but not transcript-backed participant evidence.

3. **Moderator contamination risk**: `HWAD3AR` and `HWAD6AR` contain flowing text where facilitator material may be harder to isolate than in the cleaner structured transcripts.

4. **Professional voice dominance**: The clearest Day 3 evidence is strongly provider- and professional-heavy even when speakers also reflect on their own adult lives and burdens.

5. **Question-boundary asymmetry**: `HWAD4AR` and `HWAD10AR` preserve the clearest full Q1-Q7 movement, while other transcripts need more note support to secure Part 2 boundaries.

6. **Warm-up contamination risk in English transcript**: `HWAD10AR` begins with non-guide icebreaker material, so coding must not treat the whole transcript as if it began immediately at Q1.

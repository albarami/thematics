# CASE_D3 Working Codebook

## Code families and definitions

### Family 1: Adult wellbeing conceptualisation

| Code | Definition | Example indicators |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| balance_multidimensional | Wellbeing framed as balance or integration across multiple pillars or life domains rather than a single health state | توازن, balance, holistic, pillars, integrated, wholeness |
| contentment_acceptance | Wellbeing defined through acceptance, sufficiency, gratitude, or being at peace with one’s circumstances | رضا, قناعة, acceptance, contentment, okay with life |
| inner_peace_stability | Wellness described as calm, steadiness, peace of mind, or internal psychological settling | طمأنينة, راحة نفسية, peace, calm, stability |
| functionality_coping_capacity | Being well described through the ability to function, cope, carry responsibilities, or continue daily life under pressure | cope, functioning, manage stress, carry on, daily life |
| spiritual_moral_anchor | Spirituality, faith, prayer, or moral orientation described as foundational to wellbeing rather than a marginal add-on | الله, prayer, faith, الروحية, الدين, العبادة |
| values_action_alignment | Wellbeing framed through congruence between beliefs, values, behaviour, and the kind of person one is trying to be | values and actions, aligned, behavior, principle, consistency |
| relational_connectedness | Wellbeing tied to meaningful connection, belonging, reciprocity, or supportive relationships | connected, relationships, support, belonging, around you, people |

### Family 2: Adult roles, pressures, and lived context

| Code | Definition | Example indicators |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| adult_role_responsibility | Adult life described through responsibility for self, family, work, or wider social obligations | responsibility, burden, caring for others, adult roles, obligations |
| family_caregiving_ecology | Adult wellbeing linked to family life, caregiving, parenting, marriage, or household dynamics | family, parents, children, marriage, caregiving, home |
| work_life_strain | Pressure arising from workload, schedules, dual commitments, or trying to balance employment with other adult roles | workload, shift, schedule, pressure, job, duties, work-life |
| self_responsibility_orientation | Speakers emphasise personal responsibility, self-awareness, or beginning change from the individual self | start with yourself, مسؤوليتي, self-awareness, responsibility for self |
| system_responsibility_orientation | Speakers emphasise that adult wellbeing depends on social, institutional, or systemic conditions beyond individual effort | system, institutions, policies, structures, society, pathways |
| hidden_distress_masking | Distress described as concealed, misread, or hidden behind appearances, functioning, or silence | hidden, not obvious, masking, looks fine, inside, surface |
| provider_self_as_adult | Healthcare or professional participants speak explicitly from their own adult lived burden rather than only from detached expertise | as a provider, for us too, our wellbeing, my own life, as adults |

### Family 3: Care interaction, trust, and communication

| Code | Definition | Example indicators |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| trust_questioning_barrier | Adults describe difficulty asking questions, challenging authority, or trusting the clinical exchange enough to participate fully | ask questions, afraid to ask, trust, offended, authority, challenge |
| health_literacy_navigation | Wellbeing and care quality linked to information access, understanding, explanation, and the ability to navigate services | literacy, explain, understand, informed, navigate, ask for clarification |
| patient_provider_relationship | Wellbeing shaped by the quality of the relationship between the person seeking care and the healthcare professional | relationship, rapport, trust with doctor, communication with patient |
| empathy_reassurance | Humane interaction, reassurance, emotional containment, or attentive listening described as clinically important | reassure, empathy, humane, contain, calm, explanation |
| consultation_time_pressure | Limited appointment time or rushed encounters prevent whole-person adult care | no time, rushed, 30 minutes, pressure, limited consultation |
| authority_defensiveness | Providers or institutions react defensively when patients ask questions or try to participate | defensive, offended, don't like questions, authority threatened |
| stigma_help_seeking_barrier | Stigma, shame, or fear limits help-seeking or disclosure, especially around mental health | stigma, shame, taboo, embarrassment, mental health barrier |
| language_cultural_mismatch | Communication quality weakened by language barriers, multilingual complexity, or lack of cultural fit | language, Arabic, English, multilingual, culture, translation |

### Family 4: Service fragmentation and redesign

| Code | Definition | Example indicators |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| fragmented_service_pathway | Services described as disconnected, difficult to navigate, or poorly coordinated across specialties or sectors | fragmented, silo, no pathway, no connection, referral gap, coordination |
| interdisciplinary_integration | Opportunity or need framed through stronger links across specialties, teams, disciplines, or sectors | team, integration, collaboration, interdisciplinary, between departments |
| awareness_outreach | Public or institutional awareness work proposed to increase understanding of wellbeing, rights, or available support | awareness, campaign, outreach, education, public information |
| differentiated_service_design | Calls for service models that allocate time, access, or structures differently for different adult needs or complexity levels | longer appointments, separate clinics, differentiated care, targeted design |
| digital_admin_relief | Technology or AI proposed to reduce documentation burden, improve navigation, or free clinician time | AI, technology, documentation, digital tool, admin burden |
| top_down_system_change | Need for policy-level, regulatory, leadership-level, or ministry-level action rather than only frontline adaptation | policy, leadership, ministry, mandatory, institutional change, regulation |
| healthcare_worker_wellbeing | Adult care improvement linked to workforce support, provider wellbeing, burnout prevention, or staff care structures | staff wellbeing, burnout, support for providers, worker wellbeing |
| practical_recommendation | Concrete actionable suggestion that does not fit a narrower implementation code but clearly proposes change | suggest, recommendation, propose, solution, practical step |

| general_response | Substantive material that contributes meaningfully but is not adequately indexed by a narrower code | residual substantive content |

## Codebook notes
- Codes are not mutually exclusive. A single segment may carry multiple codes.
- This is a working codebook for the Day 3 coding phase, not a final thematic structure.
- Code families organize recurring areas of attention but do not predetermine final themes.
- `moderator_context` may be used to retain discussion-flow context, but moderator-coded material must not be treated as participant evidence.
- `general_response` remains available for substantive material that cannot be responsibly reduced to a narrower retrieval code during first-pass coding.

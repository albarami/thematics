# CASE_D3 Candidate Theme Development

## Development process
Candidate themes were developed through iterative engagement with the current promoted Day 3 evidence base:
1. Familiarisation across the Day 3 transcript and note corpus with source-sensitivity constraints recorded before theme work
2. Review of the promoted `CASE_D3_coded_segments.csv` layer and its linked Gate 3 summary artifacts
3. Interpretive clustering by analytic function, question spread, speaker-role distinction, and source sensitivity rather than keyword repetition alone
4. Boundary-testing against the Day 3 coding-integrity caveats, especially weak `HWAD1AR` `Q2`/`Q3` boundaries, weak `HWAD10AR` `Q6` preservation, later moderator-led summary structure, and the substantial `unclear` material in `HWAD3AR` and `HWAD6AR`
5. Separation of transcript participant evidence from moderator context, with note files and the recommendation workbook left outside the theme-generating evidence layer

## Candidate themes tested

### Candidate A — Adult wellbeing as integrated balance, moral-spiritual grounding, and workable coping
- **Status**: RETAINED as Theme 1
- **Core idea**: Across Arabic and English Day 3 sources, adult wellbeing is described as more than symptom absence. Participants repeatedly combine balance across life domains, contentment, moral-spiritual grounding, inner steadiness, alignment between values and actions, and the practical ability to cope with adult pressures. The Day 3 logic is not merely that many pillars exist, but that adults experience wellbeing when these dimensions can be held together under real strain.
- **Supporting codes**: `balance_multidimensional`, `contentment_acceptance`, `inner_peace_stability`, `functionality_coping_capacity`, `spiritual_moral_anchor`, `values_action_alignment`, `relational_connectedness`
- **Evidence spread**: Q1-Q3 primary, with conceptual echoes continuing into Q4-Q5 as participants judge whether healthcare systems support this ideal. Strongest support comes from `HWAD10AR`, `HWAD4AR`, and `HWAD1AR`, with supportive but attribution-cautious material in `HWAD3AR`.
- **Analytical sharpness**: This is not a generic "wellbeing has many dimensions" theme. It captures a distinct adult logic in which balance, faith or value orientation, and coping capacity are treated as interdependent conditions of a workable adult life.

### Candidate B — Adult wellbeing as negotiated through family, work, caregiving, and role-bearing strain
- **Status**: RETAINED as Theme 2
- **Core idea**: Day 3 participants rarely describe adulthood as a neutral life stage. Adult wellbeing is framed through caring for children, managing households, carrying professional obligations, navigating pressure at work, and negotiating what belongs to the individual versus what belongs to the wider system. The recurring logic is that adult wellbeing is lived through responsibility, role strain, and unequal social conditions, not simply through inner attitude alone.
- **Supporting codes**: `adult_role_responsibility`, `family_caregiving_ecology`, `work_life_strain`, `self_responsibility_orientation`, `system_responsibility_orientation`, `provider_self_as_adult`
- **Evidence spread**: Q1-Q5 primary. Strongest support comes from `HWAD10AR` and `HWAD4AR`, with narrower but important supporting material from `HWAD1AR` and attribution-cautious support from `HWAD3AR`.
- **Analytical sharpness**: This theme is not just "adults have responsibilities." It captures how family, work, caregiving, and institutional conditions combine to shape what adults can sustain, disclose, or repair.

### Candidate C — Hidden strain behind adult competence and surface functioning
- **Status**: ABSORBED as cross-cutting analytical pattern
- **Core idea**: Several Day 3 sources warn that adults may appear capable, successful, religiously grounded, or professionally functional while carrying concealed strain, unresolved family burdens, or private distress. Surface functioning does not reliably show whether adult wellbeing is actually secure.
- **Supporting codes**: `hidden_distress_masking`, `functionality_coping_capacity`, `stigma_help_seeking_barrier`
- **Evidence spread**: Strongest in Q1-Q3, with later echoes in Q4-Q5 discussions about rushed systems missing what patients or providers do not say openly.
- **Reason for absorption**: The pattern is analytically important, but the Day 3 evidence shows it operating inside the conceptual wellbeing theme, the role-bearing strain theme, and the care-barrier theme rather than standing clearly on its own.

### Candidate D — Holistic adult care undermined by time pressure, hierarchy, navigation gaps, and uneven communication
- **Status**: RETAINED as Theme 3
- **Core idea**: Participants describe a persistent mismatch between the holistic language of adult wellbeing and the actual conditions of care. Adults face short appointments, waiting and access problems, uneven referral clarity, authority-sensitive interactions, unequal health literacy, cultural-language mismatch, and systems that still prioritize throughput or the physical complaint over whole-person understanding. The issue is not merely inconvenience; it is a patterned weakening of adult-supportive care.
- **Supporting codes**: `trust_questioning_barrier`, `health_literacy_navigation`, `patient_provider_relationship`, `empathy_reassurance`, `consultation_time_pressure`, `authority_defensiveness`, `stigma_help_seeking_barrier`, `language_cultural_mismatch`, `fragmented_service_pathway`
- **Evidence spread**: Q3-Q7 primary. Strongest support comes from `HWAD10AR` and `HWAD4AR`, with narrower Arabic participant support from `HWAD1AR` and explicit `unclear` caution around later `HWAD6AR` material.
- **Analytical sharpness**: This is not a loose list of barriers. It captures how time, hierarchy, explanation, navigation, and linguistic fit shape whether adults can participate meaningfully in care.

### Candidate E — Coordinated, access-aware, and institutionally embedded routes to adult-care redesign
- **Status**: RETAINED as Theme 4
- **Core idea**: When Day 3 participants turn to opportunities and recommendations, they produce concrete redesign logic rather than vague improvement talk. Recurrent proposals include better interdisciplinary coordination, stronger public awareness, differentiated access, workforce support, policy-level embedding, training, and technology that frees time or improves navigation. The central claim is that adult wellbeing cannot be integrated into healthcare by goodwill alone; it has to be designed into institutions and access pathways.
- **Supporting codes**: `interdisciplinary_integration`, `awareness_outreach`, `differentiated_service_design`, `digital_admin_relief`, `top_down_system_change`, `healthcare_worker_wellbeing`, `practical_recommendation`
- **Evidence spread**: Q4-Q7 primary. Strongest support comes from `HWAD10AR` and `HWAD4AR`, with explicit but attribution-cautious Q7 implementation material from `HWAD6AR` and narrower challenge/opportunity support from `HWAD1AR` / `HWAD3AR`.
- **Analytical sharpness**: Day 3 change talk is specific: integration, referral clarity, campaigns, provider training, evening clinics, time redesign, language access, and embedding the pillars into institutional routines.

### Candidate F — Provider-heavy and attribution-uneven adult evidence as an analysis condition
- **Status**: NOT a theme — documented as methodological limitation
- **Core idea**: The cleanest Day 3 participant evidence is strongly provider- and professional-heavy, while some of the richest later implementation material is carried by `unclear` or moderator-shaped transcript structure.
- **Reason for exclusion**: This is a property of the dataset and source recoverability, not a participant-generated substantive pattern. It should remain explicit in the theme layer, but it should not be elevated into a final theme.

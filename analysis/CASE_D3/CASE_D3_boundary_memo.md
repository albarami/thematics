# CASE_D3 Boundary Memo

## Analytic boundaries for CASE_D3

### Case boundary
- This analysis covers `CASE_D3` (Day 3 — Adults) only.
- No cross-case comparisons with `CASE_D1`, `CASE_D2`, or `CASE_D4` are permitted at this stage.
- No whole-project synthesis is attempted.
- `Day5_PolicyMakers` / `CASE_D5` is outside the approved assessment scope and must not be reintroduced into CASE_D3 materials.

### Source boundary
- **Primary verbal data**: 5 transcripts (`HWAD1AR`, `HWAD3AR`, `HWAD4AR`, `HWAD6AR`, `HWAD10AR`)
- **Contextual triangulation**: 11 note files (`HWAD0NT1`, `HWAD1NT1`, `HWAD1NT2`, `HWAD3NT1`, `HWAD5NT1`, `HWAD7NT1`, `HWAD9NT1`, `HWAD10NT1`, `HWAD10NTI`, `HWAD11NT2`, `HWAD11NT3`)
- **Auxiliary recommendation material**: `Health_Workshop_Suggestions Day 3.xlsx` (Q6-Q7 support only, explicitly labeled)
- **Excluded**: No Day 5 files, no cross-case materials, and no recommendation rows treated as transcript-equivalent evidence

### Speaker-type boundary
- **participant**: Substantive contributor whose turns count toward coding, theme support, quotation diversity, and participant-level reporting
- **moderator**: Facilitator / interviewer / session manager. Context only. Must never count as participant evidence, quotation support, participant summary content, theme evidence, or prominence support
- **note_taker_summary**: Note-taker condensation or structured summary. Contextual triangulation only. Not verbatim participant speech
- **unclear**: Substantive speech preserved without recoverable speaker identity or role. Can be coded with caution but does not count toward participant diversity metrics

### Evidence hierarchy
1. **Transcript participant speech** — strongest evidence for codes, quotations, question evidence, and theme development
2. **Transcript unclear-speaker content** — usable with attribution caveat where substantive content is recoverable
3. **Note-taker summaries** — contextual and structural support; useful for question boundaries and role triangulation, but not independent theme-generating evidence
4. **Auxiliary recommendation workbook** — supplementary Q6-Q7 recommendation support only; not theme-generating evidence
5. **Moderator speech** — context for discussion flow, question structure, and facilitation; never participant evidence

### Question boundary
- The case uses the canonical seven-question Day 3 structure (`Q1-Q7`) documented in `CASE_D3_question_map.md`
- Analysis may later be written in a question-led format, but question-to-theme claims must remain evidence-based
- If a question boundary is weak or blended in a source, that limitation must be recorded rather than smoothed over
- Recommendation workbook material may support Q6/Q7 only and must remain explicitly labeled as auxiliary

### Theme-development boundary
- No final theme claims are locked at the familiarisation stage
- Later themes must emerge from CASE_D3 evidence only and must not import Day 1 or Day 2 theme names, narrative structure, or cross-case expectations
- Strong repeated ideas observed in familiarisation are provisional analytic cues, not findings
- No theme claim may be supported by moderator prompts or recommendation-workbook content alone

### Reporting boundary
- The final CASE_D3 report must remain within the adult stakeholder context
- No claims about all days, all stakeholders, or the project as a whole are permitted at the case-analysis stage
- Professional/provider voice dominance and uneven attribution quality must be acknowledged where relevant
- Mixed-language evidence must preserve source-language authority rather than flattening all material into a single language register

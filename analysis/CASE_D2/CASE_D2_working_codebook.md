# CASE_D2 Working Codebook

## Code families and definitions

### Family 1: Wellbeing conceptualisation

| Code | Definition | Example indicators |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| contentment_acceptance | Wellbeing or wellness defined through contentment, acceptance, gratitude, or being at peace with one’s circumstances | قناعة, رضا, contentment, satisfied, okay with what you are |
| balance_multidimensional | Wellbeing framed as balance or integration across multiple dimensions of life rather than a single health state | توازن, balance, all pillars, equilibrium, holistic |
| spiritual_moral_anchor | Spirituality, faith, or moral orientation described as foundational to wellbeing rather than a marginal add-on | الله, prayer, faith, spiritual, الروحية, القضاء والقدر |
| inner_peace_stability | Wellbeing described as calm, peace of mind, steadiness, or psychological settledness | سلام داخلي, peace of mind, calm, steady, طمأنينة |
| support_as_wellbeing_core | Social or relational support treated as central to being well | support system, family support, circle, people around you, علاقات |
| functionality_self_management | Wellness described through the ability to function, cope, care for oneself, work, or maintain daily life | functioning, able to work, depend on yourself, cope, regulate |

### Family 2: Youth social ecology

| Code | Definition | Example indicators |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| family_support_ecology | Youth wellbeing discussed through family support, parenting, household stability, or family absence | family, parents, أهل, support at home, family relations |
| peer_belonging_isolation | Youth wellbeing linked to friendships, belonging, loneliness, isolation, or lack of connection | friends, isolated, alone, belonging, social circle |
| youth_transition_pressure | Stress linked to exams, university, work entry, performance expectations, or life-stage transitions | exams, study pressure, university, adolescence, transition |
| digital_social_media_pressure | Youth wellbeing shaped by digital life, online comparison, overexposure, or social-media habits | social media, phones, online, comparison, technology pressure |
| youth_voice_visibility | Explicit attention to whether youth voices are heard directly or represented by adults/professionals | youth perspective, student voice, service recipient, speaking for youth |
| intergenerational_disconnect | Youth experiences framed through weak communication or distance between young people and adults/families | generation gap, weak communication, no one listens, family distance |

### Family 3: Healthcare-system barriers and strain

| Code | Definition | Example indicators |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| consultation_time_pressure | Limited appointment time or rushed encounters prevent holistic care or deeper understanding | 30 minutes, rushed, no time, limited consultation, ضيق الوقت |
| quantity_over_quality | Institutional emphasis on throughput, numbers, or productivity over person-centered care quality | quantity, numbers, targets, KPI, more patients than quality |
| stigma_help_seeking_barrier | Stigma, shame, or fear prevents people from disclosing distress or seeking support, especially mental-health support | stigma, embarrassment, shame, not open, mental health taboo |
| confidentiality_trust_barrier | Concerns about privacy, exposure, or trust undermine help-seeking or honest disclosure | confidentiality, privacy, trust, small society, exposure |
| language_cultural_mismatch | Communication or care quality weakened by language barriers or lack of cultural fit between provider and patient | language barrier, culture, melting pot, don’t know the language |
| fragmented_service_pathway | Services described as disconnected, poorly coordinated, or difficult to navigate across specialties or stages of care | referral gaps, disconnected services, silo, no coordination |
| access_delay_waiting | Waiting times, delayed appointments, or delayed test/report pathways weaken care and wellbeing | waiting months, delayed access, appointment backlog, long queue |
| environment_design_stress | Physical service environment either worsens or eases distress, anxiety, or comfort | waiting room, colors, music, environment, clinic design |

| Code | Definition | Example indicators |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| communication_training_need | Need to train providers or wider staff in communication, empathy, and patient interaction | staff training, communication training, empathy, تعامل |
| interdisciplinary_integration | Opportunity framed through stronger links across specialties, teams, or sectors | interdisciplinary, collaboration, referral, team-based, integration |
| awareness_outreach | Public or institutional awareness work proposed to increase understanding of wellbeing and available support | awareness, campaigns, emails, outreach, توعية |
| youth_specific_service_design | Calls for services, clinics, pathways, or spaces designed specifically for youth or adolescents | youth clinic, adolescent service, age-appropriate, targeted pathway |
| family_inclusive_care | Proposals that integrate parents, caregivers, or family accompaniment into care processes | involve family, parents in care, caregiver role, family support |
| healthcare_worker_wellbeing | Improvement proposals that begin with staff wellbeing, burnout prevention, or workforce support | staff wellbeing, burnout, support for providers, employee engagement |
| environmental_design_improvement | Suggestions about the built environment, waiting areas, sensory calm, or space design as part of wellbeing | calmer space, music, colors, comfortable chair, design solution |
| practical_recommendation | Concrete actionable proposal that does not fit a narrower implementation code but clearly suggests change | suggestion, recommendation, propose, solution |
| top_down_system_change | Need for policy-level, leadership-level, or management-level action rather than only frontline adaptation | leadership, management, ministry, top-down, policy |

### Family 5: Service experience and care quality

| Code | Definition | Example indicators |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| whole_person_care | Care framed as attending to the full person rather than only the presenting symptom or body part | whole person, not just the disease, holistic care |
| empathy_reassurance | Clinical value placed on reassurance, emotional support, calm explanation, and humane interaction | reassure, calm the patient, explanation, support emotionally |
| patient_provider_relationship | Wellbeing shaped by the quality of the relationship between the provider and the person seeking care | relationship, rapport, trust with doctor, communication with patient |
| self_disclosure_hidden_distress | Distress framed as partially hidden, hard to disclose, or easy to miss if staff only ask surface questions | hidden, not obvious, probe more, surface looks fine |
| service_recipient_comparison | Service users compare good and bad encounters to illustrate what wellbeing-supportive care feels like | one doctor helped, another dismissed, comparison of encounters |

## Codebook notes
- Codes are not mutually exclusive. A single segment may carry multiple codes.
- This is a working codebook for the Day 2 coding phase, not a final thematic structure.
- Code families organize recurring areas of attention but do not predetermine final themes.
- `moderator_context` may be used to retain discussion-flow context, but moderator-coded material must not be treated as participant evidence.
- `general_response` remains available for substantive material that cannot be responsibly reduced to a narrower retrieval code during first-pass coding.

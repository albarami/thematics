## Translation handling rules

### Arabic as authoritative where Arabic is original
Arabic is the authoritative source language for the five Arabic transcripts and most note files. Where Arabic text is quoted or coded, the Arabic remains the primary evidentiary form. English glosses or translations are interpretive aids for reporting only.

### English sources are natively authoritative in their own right
`HWYO9AR` and `HWYO10AR` are natively English sources. Their wording should be quoted directly, without back-translation into Arabic for analysis purposes. Any Arabic summary of these sources is secondary to the English wording.

### Mixed-language handling
Some Day 2 Arabic transcripts contain embedded English terms such as `wellbeing`, `support system`, `ups and downs`, `clinical psychologist`, `GP pediatrics`, and similar professional phrases. These should not be normalised away during coding. The mixed-language form is part of how participants actually articulated the concepts.

### Key Arabic concepts preserved
The following Arabic terms carry culturally and spiritually specific meaning and should be preserved in Arabic with glosses where needed:

| Arabic term | Transliteration | Working gloss | Why preserved |
|------------|----------------|---------------|---------------|
| الحياة الطيبة | al-hayat al-tayyiba | the good / wholesome life | Carries moral-spiritual resonance beyond a thin English wellbeing label |
| العافية | al-'afiya | wellness / wholeness / being well | Often exceeds narrow biomedical health and includes spiritual and social dimensions |
| رضا | rida | acceptance / contentment | Often linked to divine decree and moral stance, not only subjective satisfaction |
| قناعة | qana'a | contentment / sufficiency | A spiritually inflected idea of enoughness, not mere preference satisfaction |
| ركائز | raka'iz | pillars | Refers to the five HT pillars as an integrated framework |
| المحاور | al-muhawir | interviewer / facilitator | Important moderator role label in Arabic transcripts |
| مدير الجلسة | mudir al-jalsa | session manager | Explicit moderator/facilitator role label |
| متلقي خدمة | mutalaqqi khidma | service recipient | Important participant-role marker for non-provider voices |

### Note-file language asymmetry
The note files are not literal translations of transcript speech. Even where a note file is in English for an Arabic or mixed-language discussion, it should be treated as a note-taker summary, not as a transcript translation. The note file may clarify structure, role labels, or question coverage, but it does not override the original transcript wording.

### Recommendation workbook language handling
The recommendation workbook may contain Arabic and/or mixed-language entries, but because it is auxiliary structured material rather than transcript evidence, language issues here are handled under the recommendation-usage rule rather than under quotation authority.

### Reporting convention
In later reporting outputs:
- Arabic quotations should be presented in Arabic with English translation or gloss where needed
- English quotations from `HWYO9AR` and `HWYO10AR` should be presented directly
- Mixed-language wording should be preserved where analytically meaningful
- The source language should be recorded for every quotation or excerpt used
- If an English note summary is used to support context for an Arabic source, it must be labeled as note-taker summary material rather than participant quotation

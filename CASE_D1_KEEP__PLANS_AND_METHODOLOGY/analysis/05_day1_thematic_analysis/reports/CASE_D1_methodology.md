# CASE_D1 Methodology
 
 ## Purpose
 This document sets out the methodology used for the CASE_D1 analysis.
 
 This methodology is specific to CASE_D1 as a Day 1 childhood stakeholder case. It defines the methodological approach, the data basis, the analytic procedure, and the measures used to support transparency and consistency.
 
 ## Methodological approach
 The analysis uses a within-case reflexive thematic analysis approach.
 
 This means the work focuses on identifying patterned meaning within the CASE_D1 material rather than counting responses or testing a fixed framework. Coding and theme development are interpretive and iterative. The discussion guide is used to preserve the sequence of the conversation, but the final themes are developed from the data rather than copied from the guide questions.

## Data used
The analysis used prepared Day 1 materials for CASE_D1 only.

### Primary verbal data
- 7 Day 1 transcript files as the primary verbal data base

 ### Contextual and supporting data
 - 8 Day 1 note files as contextual and triangulation support
 - 1 structured Day 1 recommendation workbook as auxiliary recommendation-layer material only
 
 Transcripts were treated as the primary verbal data. Notes were used to clarify, support, and cross-check the transcript material. Where the two source types differed in level of detail, the transcript material remained primary. The structured recommendation workbook was not treated as transcript-equivalent evidence and was not used to generate themes.
 
 ## Analytic procedure
 ### 1. Set the analytic boundary
 The analysis is bounded to CASE_D1 as a single Day 1 childhood stakeholder case. This boundary keeps the interpretation focused on case-specific patterned meaning within the Day 1 material.
 
 ### 2. Re-read the prepared sources
 The CASE_D1 prepared materials are re-read closely to build familiarity with the content, tone, repeated concerns, and differences across sources. This stage helps identify early patterns, recurring concepts, and areas where the note-based material is more compressed than the transcript-derived material.
 
 ### 3. Preserve discussion context without turning questions into themes
 A Day 1 question map was created so that excerpts could be read in relation to the discussion sequence. This helped preserve the movement from definitions of wellbeing, to the role of the pillars, to service reflection, barriers, opportunities, and suggestions.
 
 The question map is used as a contextual aid only. It does not determine the themes. Moderator prompts and discussion-guide wording are treated as contextual framing only and are not used as participant evidence for theme development. Where a line is uncertain or question-like, it is retained as context rather than counted as participant evidence. The map is reconciled against the reviewed discussion guide so that the question structure remains accurate while thematic interpretation remains stable.
 
 ### 4. Code the material into meaning units
 The prepared CASE_D1 sources are segmented into coded meaning units. These segments capture short pieces of data that express a relevant idea, concern, tension, or proposed solution.
 
 Coding is interpretive rather than purely descriptive. Some codes stay close to participant wording, while others capture the underlying meaning of a segment. The coded material is organised into the CASE_D1 coded-segment dataset and then consolidated into a working codebook.
 
 ### 5. Write reflexive analytic memos during coding
 Reflexive memos are written during the coding process to document decisions, uncertainties, and methodological boundaries. These memos cover:
 - coding reflection
 - source sensitivity
 - language and translation handling
 - analytic boundaries
 
 These memos help keep the analysis transparent and consistent. They also document where note-based compression, mixed-language material, or boundary risks need special care.
 
 ### 6. Develop candidate themes and refine them into final themes
 After coding, related patterns are grouped into candidate themes. These candidate themes are then reviewed and refined to test whether they represent a coherent patterned meaning across the CASE_D1 material.
 
 The final themes are not produced by simply collecting similar topics. They are developed by asking whether different excerpts express a broader shared meaning and whether that meaning matters for understanding the Day 1 case as a whole.

 This refinement process resulted in four final CASE_D1 themes:
 1. Wellbeing is built through balanced contentment, safety, and morally grounded steadiness
 2. Childhood wellbeing depends on an interdependent support ecology around children
 3. Childhood wellbeing support is undermined by fragmented, strained, and uneven service conditions
 4. Culturally grounded coordination, early awareness, and formal implementation are imagined routes to change
 
 ### 7. Check the themes against the full CASE_D1 artifact set
 The refined themes are checked for consistency against the supporting CASE_D1 materials, including the excerpt bank, question map, candidate theme file, salience matrix, and reporting documents. This step helps ensure that the final write-up remains aligned with the established case evidence and within-case boundary.
 
 ### 8. Add a supplementary prominence layer after theme development
 A Day 1 salience or prominence table is produced only after the themes have already been developed. This is a supplementary descriptive layer, not a theme-generation method.
 
 Its purpose is to show which retained final themes are more central, broader, or more concentrated within the CASE_D1 discussion. It does not prove theme validity and it does not decide which themes count as final.

## How language and translation were handled
The CASE_D1 material included mixed-language content. Original-language excerpts remained authoritative throughout the analysis. Selective English support was used only where needed to make excerpts readable in reporting.

No translated derivative dataset was created to replace the original material. This helped preserve meaning as closely as possible while still allowing clear reporting.

 ## How quality and transparency were maintained
 The analysis is documented across multiple CASE_D1 artifacts, including:
 - coded segments
 - a working codebook
 - candidate theme development records
 - a final theme file
 - an excerpt bank
 - a salience matrix
 - reflexive memos
 - a guide reconciliation note
 - a CSV validation report
 - processing log and audit-trail entries
 
 These materials support transparency, auditability, and consistency across the case analysis. They also allow moderator or question-like framing to be separated from participant evidence in later reporting outputs.

 ## What this methodology was designed to do
 This methodology was designed to answer three linked questions within CASE_D1:
 - how Day 1 participants understood wellbeing
 - how they connected the five HT pillars to childhood support
 - how they described the relationship between that model of wellbeing and existing healthcare services
 
 It is also designed to preserve interpretive depth while staying transparent about source limits, mixed-language handling, and the difference between theme development and supplementary prominence judgement.
 
 ## Scope and limitations
 This methodology is limited to one case only. It supports interpretation within CASE_D1 and should be read as a single-case Day 1 methodology.
 
 Some note-derived materials were compressed, and some question-context attribution remained partly inferential where the prepared material preserved meaning more strongly than conversational sequence. Mixed-language material also carries unavoidable meaning risk, even with selective English support.
 
 ## Summary
 In practical terms, the CASE_D1 methodology involves:
 1. defining a strict within-case Day 1 boundary
 2. re-reading the prepared sources
 3. mapping the discussion context
 4. coding meaning units
 5. memoing interpretive and methodological decisions
 6. developing and refining candidate themes into final themes
 7. checking consistency across the CASE_D1 artifact set
 8. adding a supplementary prominence layer after theme development
 
 This methodology provides a transparent, case-bounded, reflexive thematic procedure for analysing CASE_D1.
